Nature as a composer

Readers of musical biographies know that many composers have been passionate lovers of nature. Beethoven confessed that he often preferred the company of a tree to that of a man, and some of his best ideas came to him on his daily outdoor walks (the "Pastoral" Symphony among them). Mozart composed everywhere from a stage-coach to a workroom but his favorite workspace was an open garden.Some composers traveled to find inspiration. Felix Mendelssohn visited the island of Staffa on Scotland's west coast before writing the concert overture The Hebrides (also known as Fingal's Cave).
